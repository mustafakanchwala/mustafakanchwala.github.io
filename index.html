Digital ASIC Designer  ·  PhD Candidate, University of Toronto

Hi! I'm a Digital ASIC Designer and PhD candidate in the Intelligent Sensory Microsystems Lab at the University of Toronto, advised by Prof. Roman Genov.

My research focuses on low-power digital ASIC design for AI/ML accelerators, DSP pipelines, and adaptive controllers targeting event-driven and edge-inference systems for implantable neural interfaces. I have end-to-end ownership across four taped-out chips in 65 nm CMOS — from microarchitecture and RTL through synthesis, timing closure, full-chip integration, and post-silicon validation.

Results published at IEEE ISSCC, VLSI Technology and Circuits, IEEE TBioCAS, and IEEE BioCAS. Recipient of the NSERC PGS-D Scholarship for 2023–2025. You can find more information in my CV.
